Research Direction


Massive Dataset

  1. Sequence Dataset
    • Analysis and study of DNA, RNA and protein sequences to investigate their biological functions.
  2. Omics Dataset
    • The training data covers genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics, epigenetics data, mutation data and other multi-omics data;
    • Covers nucleic acids and protein sequences of nearly a hundred species.
  3. Interaction Dataset
    • Covers known and predicted interaction data between proteins, nucleic acids, and small molecules;
    • These interactions include direct (physical) and indirect (functional) associations; they come from computational predictions, knowledge transfer between organisms, and interactions aggregated from other publicly databases;
    • Involves protein and gene interactions, chemical interactions, and post-translational modifications in major model organism species.
  4. Literature Dataset
    • Track and detect cutting-edge developments in life sciences;
    • Build the full pipeline for literature information extraction;
    • Enhance information on protease experimental activity;
    • Efficiently support scientific research.

Diverse Computing Tools

  2. Gene: Nucleic Acid Sequence Phylogenetic Tree, RNA Structure Prediction;
  3. Protogene: Protein Interaction Prediction, ncRNA-Protein Interaction Prediction;
