Research Directions


Massive Datasets

  1. Sequence Data
    • Analyzing DNA, RNA and protein sequences to investigate their biological functions.
  2. Omics Data
    • Training data covering genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics, epigenetics data, mutation data and other multi-omics data;
    • Including nucleic acid and protein sequences from nearly a hundred species.
  3. Interaction Data
    • Covering known and predicted interactions between proteins, nucleic acids, and small molecules;
    • These interactions include direct (physical) and indirect (functional) associations derived from computational predictions, cross-species knowledge transfer, and data aggregated from other publicly databases;
    • Involves protein and gene interactions, chemical interactions, and post-translational modifications in major model organisms.
  4. Literature Data
    • Tracking and monitoring cutting-edge developments in life sciences;
    • Building a comprehensive pipeline for literature information extraction;
    • Enhancing information on protease experimental activities;
    • Efficiently supporting scientific research.
